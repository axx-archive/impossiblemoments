# Why Britney Will Be a Big Draw
*One Night Entertainment — Investor Memo*

**Thesis:** Britney Spears' proven Vegas audience and price point, a multi-year and reiterated refusal to perform live in the U.S., and a structurally younger Strip visitor base establish the demand conditions under which an avatar residency — benchmarked to ABBA Voyage's operating performance — can price and fill at scale.

**$137.7M** Piece of Me gross, 2013–2017 [1]  |  **$500K** per-show Caesars guarantee, Domination 2019 [1]  |  **$113M** ABBA Voyage 2024 gross, >90% occupancy [6]  |  **43.6** average Vegas visitor age 2024, down from 46.6 in 2019 [8]  |  **26–30M** Britney Spotify monthly listeners [3]  |  **1.1M** *The Woman in Me* U.S. copies, week one [4]

### §1 — Britney Has Sold This Room Before
Piece of Me ran 248 shows at The Axis at Planet Hollywood from Dec 2013 through Dec 2017, grossing **$137,695,392 on 916,184 tickets** — ~$555,000 per show at ~80% fill against a 4,600-seat house [1]. The original two-year contract was extended twice. The 2017 finale year grossed **$38.9M at a $163.20 average ticket**, the second highest-grossing North American female show that year; the NYE 2017 closing show did ~$1.2M at a $255 average [1]. Piece of Me ended as the **#3 highest-grossing Vegas residency of all time**, behind only Celine Dion and Elton John's Red Piano [2]. As late as 2019, Caesars guaranteed Britney **$500,000 per show** for Domination — a revealed willingness-to-pay at the end of her active career [1]. The inference drawn here is narrow: Piece of Me establishes the *addressable audience and price point* for a Britney product on the Strip. It does not, and is not cited to, mechanically imply that an avatar show inherits that gross.

### §2 — Still Playing Everywhere
Britney has not released a studio album since 2016 or toured since 2018, yet Spotify monthly listeners remain in the **26–30M range**, and "Toxic" has crossed **1 billion Spotify streams** [3]. Catalog sync-ability is current: "Toxic" re-entered global charts in Nov 2023 on the back of a *Saltburn* needle-drop — useful as a sync/placement signal, not as standalone demand proof [3]. Her memoir *The Woman in Me* (Gallery Books, Oct 2023) sold **1.1 million U.S. copies in its first week**, #1 on the NYT Hardcover Nonfiction list; the Michelle Williams-narrated audiobook became an Audible bestseller [4]. FX/Hulu's *Framing Britney Spears* was among Hulu's most-watched documentaries of 2021; Netflix's *Britney vs Spears* hit the U.S. Top 10 [3].

### §3 — The Only Way to See Her
Britney's refusal to perform live in the U.S. is now a multi-year, consistent pattern, not a single statement. She has not toured since 2018. In September 2022 she stated she had no interest in returning to the stage; on **January 9, 2026** she reiterated on Instagram: *"I will never perform in the U.S. again… I hope to be sitting on a stool… performing with my son… in the UK and AUSTRALIA very soon"* [5]. This is behavioral disengagement, not a contract — public figures reverse positions — but seven years of withdrawal plus an explicit, reiterated U.S.-specific refusal is the relevant signal for scarcity. An avatar residency is the only format that converts that constraint into a monetizable product.

The closest available operating comparable is **ABBA Voyage**, with material differences noted. Voyage **grossed £104.34M (~$113M) on 1.06M tickets across 374 performances at >90% occupancy in 2024**; cumulative through 2023: >1.5M tickets, >$150M [6]. Voyage's venue+tech build came in at **~$175M** — cited here as a build-cost benchmark for the venue/tech line item, not as a comparable to total raise [6]. Pophouse Entertainment Fund I raised **€1.2B (~$1.3B)** and acquired the KISS catalog for >$300M in 2024; institutional capital now treats this as a repeatable asset class [6]. Differences to underwrite against: ABBA had four living cooperating members, Mamma Mia! tailwind, London's tourism base, and a first-generation tech build. Offsetting, volumetric capture and real-time avatar rendering have materially advanced since 2022, and the ABBA Voyage generation of shows (purpose-built venue, full-length set, cooperating estate, real-time rendering) is categorically distinct from the earlier underperforming Orbison/Houston/Tupac hologram tours that toured legacy venues as segments of mixed bills.

### §4 — Her Audience Just Got Younger
The LVCVA 2024 Visitor Profile Study documents a structural age reset on the Strip: **millennials are now 46% of Las Vegas visitors**, the 30–39 bracket alone is 35%, average visitor age fell from **46.6 (2019) to 43.6 (2024)**, and the 65+ share collapsed from **19% to 6%** [8]. Against a 41.7M annual visitor base, that is the exact demographic that bought Piece of Me tickets in 2013–2017, now a decade into peak earnings. Millennials born 1985–1995 enter the BLS 35–54 peak-earnings band in 2026; aggregate millennial net worth expanded from **$3.93T (2019) to $15.25T (Q2 2024)** per Federal Reserve DFA [7]. LVCVA also reports the $100K+ household-income share of visitors rising from 28% (2019) to 64% (2024), but that figure is partly a nominal-dollar artifact — the 2019 $100K threshold is ~$83K in 2024 dollars — so the age shift, not the income shift, is the load-bearing data point [8]. Pollstar Top 100 average ticket hit **$135.92 in 2024, +28% over two years**, and Live Nation FY2024 concerts AOI grew +65% YoY on 151M fans — the broader live category is pricing up into exactly this buyer [9].

### §5 — Risk Register and Pointers
- **Avatar-format substitution risk.** Live-artist demand does not mechanically convert to avatar demand; ABBA Voyage is the only operating full-show precedent at scale.
- **No second Strip precedent.** The avatar residency format has no second completed Strip comparable; Voyage is London, purpose-built, and ABBA-specific.
- **Artist-statement reversal risk.** Britney has reversed public statements before; the scarcity thesis rests on multi-year behavior, not a contract.
- **Data provenance.** The $137.7M Piece of Me figure is Billboard Boxscore, not independently audited [1]; streaming and memoir figures in §2 are subject to standard fact-check prior to publication [3][4].
